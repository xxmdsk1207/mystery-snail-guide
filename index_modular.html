What Is a Mystery Snail?

Mystery Snails (Pomacea diffusa, formerly P. bridgesii) are peaceful freshwater gastropods prized for their active personality, stunning color morphs, and algae-cleaning abilities.

  • Family: Ampullariidae (Apple Snail family)
  • Origin: South America, Amazon River basin
  • Breathing: Both gills (aquatic) + lung (aerial)
  • Unique: Lays eggs above waterline — no other aquatic snail does this
Habitat Profile

Natural Environment

In the wild, mystery snails inhabit slow-moving rivers, ponds, and marshes with dense vegetation. They're highly adaptable but thrive in warm, moderately hard water.

Module 06 · Breeding & Reproduction

Breeding & Reproduction

Sexing, clutch incubation pipeline, and colony population management.

Incubation Pipeline

Phase 1 · Day 0

Clutch Deposition

Females typically lay pink, cocoon-like clutches 2–4 hours after dark, above the waterline in humid areas. Each clutch can contain 50–200 eggs. Lower the water level 8–10 cm to give the female space. Increase feeding 2 weeks prior to encourage laying.

Phase 2 · Days 1–14

Humidity Saturation

Maintain 80%+ humidity without direct water contact to prevent drowning or drying. Keep the lid slightly ajar or mist the glass near the clutch lightly every 2 days. At 26°C, expect hatching around day 16–18.

Phase 3 · Day 14–28

Emergence Phase

Dark shells become visible as the clutch softens. Hatchlings drop into the water below. Immediately provide algae film — hatchlings eating algae in the first 8 days show 90%+ survival vs 64% on commercial food alone.

Reproductive Biology

Sexing Mystery Snails

Mystery snails are dioecious — strictly separate sexes. They cannot self-fertilize. Keep 5–6 snails to ensure a mix of sexes.

♂ Male
  • • Visible penis sheath (upper right mantle)
  • • Slightly smaller shell
  • • Identified when snail fully extends
♀ Female
  • • No sheath visible in mantle
  • • Often slightly larger
  • • Deposits eggs above waterline

Egg Identification

Fresh clutch = bright pink-magenta. Viable clutch darkens to cocoa-brown over days. Infertile or dead clutch stays pale/white and may mold. Discard moldy clutches to protect water quality.

Pro Tip: Reproductive Dry Zone

Always leave 2–4 inches of dry glass at the top of your aquarium — the "Reproductive Dry Zone." Females may crawl out or refuse to lay eggs, leading to egg-binding — a potentially fatal condition.

Controlling Population

Keep water level within 2–3 cm of the lid to eliminate the dry zone — females will not lay. Or keep only one snail. Stored sperm can fertilize multiple clutches after a single mating.

Module 07 · Shell Health & Pathologies

Shell Health &
Common Pathologies

Shell integrity is the primary bioindicator of a mystery snail's overall physiological health.

Calcium Bio-tracking

Monitor the leading edge of the shell. Translucent growth indicates calcium-deficiency or low pH. Improvement appears as opaque new shell at the aperture within 2–3 weeks of fixing water chemistry. Maintain GH above 12 to ensure continuous repair cycles.

Pinhole Prevention

Pinholes and pitting develop in acidic water. Maintain GH above 12 for continuous repair cycles. Cuttlebone, crushed coral, and Wonder Shell are the most reliable calcium supplementation methods.

Operculum Assessment

A fully sealed operculum = healthy snail at rest. Loose, detaching, or absent operculum = severe distress. Healthy snails sleep up to 13 hours/day. If a snail hasn't moved in 24+ hrs, gently smell — death produces a strong sulfur odor immediately.

Contraindicated Substances
/copper-toxic-mystery-snail/

Copper (Cu²⁺) is acutely lethal to all invertebrates, including copper sulfate compounds. Always check ingredient lists on fish medications, fertilizers, and foods.

⚠ Ich treatments, antibiotics, anti-parasitic medications
⚠ Planted tank fertilizers (trace copper micronutrients)
⚠ Fish foods containing cupric sulfate
⚠ Tap water from copper plumbing (use dechlorinator)

Common Questions · Quick Reference

Frequently Asked Questions

My snail hasn't moved in 2 days — is it dead?
Mystery snails can sleep for up to 13 hours straight. Check the operculum: if it's firmly shut, the snail is just resting. If it's detaching or the snail floats with a foul sulfur odor — it's deceased. Remove dead snails immediately as they cause large ammonia spikes.
Why is my snail's shell pitted or turning white?
Almost certainly low calcium or low pH. Shell material (aragonite) dissolves in acidic water. Fix: raise pH to 7.4–7.8, add cuttlebone, raise GH to 12–18. New shell growth appears at the aperture within weeks — old damage won't repair, but growth continues healthily.
How do I tell male from female?
Hold the snail up and look into the opening when fully extended. Males have a visible penis sheath in the upper right of the mantle cavity. Females have no such structure. It's easiest to check while the snail is climbing glass. Buy 5–6 snails to ensure a good mix of sexes.
How do I successfully hatch a mystery snail clutch?
Leave the clutch in place above the waterline. Keep the lid on tightly to maintain 80%+ humidity. Temperature 25–28°C. Hatchlings are fully independent and fall into the water. Ensure algae growth in the tank beforehand — first-week algae access raises survival from ~64% to over 90%.
Can mystery snails live with bettas / shrimp / other snails?
Safe: neon tetras, corydoras, otocinclus, ember tetras, dwarf shrimp (cherry, amano), rasboras, white cloud minnows. Risky: bettas (individual-dependent). Avoid: pufferfish, loaches, cichlids, crayfish, crabs. Other mystery snails coexist peacefully.
